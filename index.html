Primer

Before the charts: what are we comparing?

CMIP6 is a coordinated library of climate model experiments used by researchers around the world. Instead of asking for one prediction, CMIP6 asks what the climate system does under different Shared Socioeconomic Pathways, or SSPs: plausible futures built around emissions, energy choices, land use, and policy.

Model ensemble

CMIP6 gives us a controlled way to compare futures.

Each pathway is run through climate models using the same physical system. That makes the comparison meaningful: differences in warming and rainfall are tied to the pathway assumptions, not a change in chart design.

Project focus

We track heat and precipitation through 2100.

The explorer uses near-surface air temperature and daily precipitation anomalies relative to a 1995-2014 baseline, with regional summaries for the Arctic, continents, South Asia, Australia, and the global average.

SSP1-2.6

The low-warming path

A sustainability-focused future with aggressive emissions cuts. Warming is not erased, but the late-century global average stays near 1.6°C in this model run, leaving more room for adaptation.

SSP2-4.5

Middle-of-the-road drift

Current policy momentum continues with partial mitigation. The world still warms substantially, reaching about 3.0°C globally by late century, and the adaptation window narrows.

SSP3-7.0

Regional rivalry

Cooperation weakens, emissions remain high, and regional vulnerability grows. Global warming rises to roughly 5.0°C, while the Arctic warms more than twice that amount.

SSP5-8.5

Fossil-fueled development

A fossil-fuel intensive benchmark where warming is fast and large. By 2091-2100, the global mean reaches about 6.2°C above the baseline and the Arctic approaches 14.9°C.

Motivation

The stakes become visible when the same planet is viewed six ways.

Each figure below is a D3 visualization rendered from the processed CMIP6 data, designed to show why a pathway comparison matters before you enter the fully interactive globe.

Heat pattern

Warming is global, but not evenly distributed.

Under the fossil-fueled pathway, late-century warming appears across the entire map. The deepest signal is at high latitudes, where Arctic warming amplifies faster than the global average.

Temperature anomaly under SSP5-8.5 by 2100

Water pattern

Rainfall does not shift in one direction.

Precipitation responds unevenly: some regions become wetter, some become drier, and some see sharper variability. This is why climate risk cannot be understood from temperature alone.

Precipitation change under SSP5-8.5 by 2100

Pathway split

The four futures separate quickly.

The scenario lines begin near the same present, then diverge as emissions choices accumulate. By the end of the century, the difference between SSP1-2.6 and SSP5-8.5 is not a subtle modeling detail; it is a different operating climate.

Global temperature scenarios over time

Regional burden

The Arctic becomes the clearest warning signal.

Regional averages reveal the hidden geography of global warming. Every region warms under SSP5-8.5, but the Arctic is pushed farthest from its historical baseline.

Projected 2100 warming by region

Compound risk

Heat and rainfall combine into regional stress.

Warming is universal, but precipitation change is more local. The scatterplot shows which regions face the double problem of rising heat and shifting water availability.

Temperature vs. precipitation change by region

Acceleration

By decade, the pattern becomes impossible to miss.

The heatmap turns the century into a timeline. Regional warming does not arrive all at once; it intensifies decade by decade, making early pathway choices visible later in the data.

Method

Built from processed climate model data.

01

Data pipeline

CMIP6 temperature and precipitation fields were converted into web-ready CSV and JSON assets: global and regional time series, late-century summaries, gridded decade data, and a TopoJSON world basemap.

02

D3 evidence layer

The six motivation figures are rendered as D3 SVG charts from the processed data while preserving the notebook's analytical intent.

03

Interactive layer

The globe loads compact decade grids, then updates color, scenario, variable, time, and rotation in the browser so visitors can move from narrative to exploration.

Conclusion

The future is not written yet.

The core takeaway is simple but consequential: every fraction of warming avoided changes the map. In this model run, the low pathway keeps late-century global warming near 1.6°C, while the fossil-fueled benchmark climbs beyond 6°C and pushes the Arctic toward 15°C. The difference between pathways is the difference between adaptation under pressure and transformation at planetary scale.
